The Problem

Construction runs on relationships.
Your contact data doesn't.

Every project brings dozens of new contacts. Most of them never make it into your database — and when you need them again, they're buried in an inbox or gone entirely.

01

Thousands of emails, zero structure

A single commercial project generates thousands of emails weekly across GCs, subs, suppliers, and consultants. Critical contact details are trapped in inboxes, never captured.

02

Subcontractors change constantly

Estimators move between firms. Project managers get promoted. Phone numbers change. Your contact list from 18 months ago is already half-useless when you need it for the next bid.

03

CRM data entry never gets done

Your team is on-site, not at a desk logging contacts into Salesforce. CRM adoption fails in over 60% of construction firms — not because the tools are bad, but because nobody has time to feed them.

04

Bid day chaos

When a new tender lands, you need to send Invitations to Bid to 50 subcontractors immediately. If your database is outdated, emails bounce, calls go to wrong numbers, and you lose the bid.
